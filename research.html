Current research

My current research interests lie in

  • mathematical epidemiology and
  • epidemic modeling.
I use mathematical models to help public health researchers better understand how the disease is spreading, forecast the epidemic's future, and optimize the allocation of resources in mitigating this spread.  Recently, I am looking into the multi-scale models for predicting the effectiveness of mitigation efforts in controlling vector-borne epidemics, such as dengue fever, Chikungunya and Zika virus.
