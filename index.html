Hi, I'm Zhuolin (Jolene).  Welcome to my website !

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow, working with Dr. James (Mac) Hyman, in the Department of Mathematics of Tulane University in New Orleans,LA.

My current research interests include mathematical modeling of infectious diseases, computational epidemiology, uncertainty quantification and statistical learning.

I use mathematical tools to help public health researchers better understand how the disease is spreading and optimize the mitigation resources. Recently, I am looking into the multi-scale models for predicting the effectiveness of mitigation efforts in controlling mosquito-borne epidemics, such as dengue fever, chikungunya and Zika.

I earned my PhD degree in Applied Mathematics at Tulane University with Dr. Alexander Kurganov in 2016, for which I was working extensively on designing numerical methods for partial differential equations.

I also completed a Master's degree in Statistics at Tulane in 2016.
