Hi, I'm Zhuolin ("Jolene").  Welcome to my website !

I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow, working with Dr. James (Mac) Hyman, in the Department of Mathematics of Tulane University in New Orleans,LA.

I am an Applied Mathematician, researching the mathematical modeling of infectious disease, such as vector-borne diseases and sexually transmitted diseases, to help public health researchers better understand the spread of disease and optimizes mitigation resource.

My research interests include mathematical modeling of infectious diseases, computational epidemiology, uncertainty quantification and statistical learning.

I earned my PhD degree in Applied Mathematics at Tulane University with Dr. Alexander Kurganov in 2016, for which I was working extensively on designing numerical methods for partial differential equations.

I also completed a Master's degree in Statistics at Tulane in 2016.
